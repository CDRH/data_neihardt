      <span class="dateline">Branson, Mo., April 7, '26.</span>

      <span class="salute">My dear Mrs. Weston:-</span>
   
      <p>Please pardon my delay in replying to yours of March 30th. I have been swamped with work and this is my first breathing spell.</p>

      <p>I agree to accept $400 for the four lectures to be given on Dec. 4th and 11th this year, as follows:</p>

      <p>Readings from THE SONG OF THE INDIAN WARS, a part of the Epic Cycle of the West.</p>

      <p>mornings   Dec. 4. "The Beecher's Island Fight".
           Dec. 11. "The Death of Crazy Horse".</p>

      <p>Two Lectures on POETIC VALUES.</p>

      <p>evenings   Dec. 4. Science and Poetry.
           Dec. 11. The Creative Dream.</p>
      <span class="closer">
         <span class="salute">Very truly yours,</span>

         <br/>Jno. G. Neihardt
</span>
      <p>Later on, I can furnish you material for advertising, if you wish.</p>
