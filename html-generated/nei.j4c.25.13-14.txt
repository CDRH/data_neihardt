                <span class="dateline">Skyrim,  7-24-'59</span>
                
                
                <span class="salute">Dear Slade:</span>
              
                <p>Golly! I'm sorry I missed you! To think that you were in Columbia, and I was away! But you say you may return in Aug. I hope I'll not miss you then.</p>
                
                <p>Some nice things have been happening. I was happy with Alice &amp; the children in Mississippi. Then there's a party wanting an option on <u>When the Tree</u> etc. — television rights. Also, the state agency of publications in Czechoslovakia wants to publish 
                    
                    
                    
                    
                    
                    my <u>Cry of the People</u> in an anthology of American poetry. The Poetry Society of Texas wants me to be sole judge of its poetry contest. They have real poets down there — more than their proper share, it seems. I've judged several times during the past 20 or 25 years. Found a really great ballad last time. Gave it first and Readers Digest featured it.</p>
                
                <div class="note">(inside)</div>
                
                
                
                
                
                <p>I'm alone in Skyrim. It's beautiful, and <u>so damned lonely</u>. Mrs. <del>Aly</del> Aly will be here about Aug. 1st. We will <del/> make a trip into Nebr. for the biography. This should occupy the first two weeks of the month.</p>
                <span class="closer">
                <span class="salute">Affection always for <del>you</del> you and Nita.</span>
                
                <br/>John N.
                </span>
